Bar Chart: Average Age During Arrest Incident by Race in Cook County, 1983-2023

This visualization compiles data from the Cook County State Attorney's Office from the last 30 years to display the average age of offenders during the incident that led to their arrest and sentencing, separated by race. White offenders tend to be the oldest, while people of color have historically been younger. This disparity of age when becoming involved in the prison system is precisely what motivates me to advocate for marginalized communities.

Line Graph: Annual Arrests in Chicago by Race, 2014-2024

I used data from the City of Chicago's Data Portal to aggregate the number of arrests that have occurred in the past 10 years in the city by race. While arrests among the Black community have decreased since 2020, they are still significantly and alarmingly higher than any other reporting demographic; likewise, arrests among the Hispanic population have steadily increased since 2021.

Map: Chicago Homicides in Last Year

I used ward boundary data as well as crime data in the last year from the City of Chicago's Data Portal to map out Chicago's homicides in the last year within ward boundaries and according to location type (apartment, street, etc.). Looking at the map, it is clear that these homicides are concentrated in the West and South sides of the city, in which neighborhoods consist primarily of people of color and marginalized communities. Chicago's North side, predominately white, has seen significantly less homicides in the last year.

Timeline: James Soto's Wrongful Incarceration, 1981-2023

This timeline accompanies my blog post about James Soto's wrongful incarceration, using Medill's Knight Lab tools to create an interactive visualization of Soto's journey to freedom.
